**PhD student opportunity in the broad field of high-temperature geochemistry, igneous petrology, and cosmochemistry**

The ICP-MS lab at Geoscience Department, University of Nevada Las Vegas is growing. In the next two years, we are adding a multiple-collector inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometer (MC-ICP-MS), funded by NSF MRI program, and a 193 nm excimer laser ablation system, funded by NASA PME program, to our existing iCAPTM Qc ICP-MS. I am looking for a highly motivated PhD student to work with me in the broad field of high-temperature geochemistry, igneous petrology, and cosmochemistry, and to help me manage the above-mentioned analytical facilities. This is a state-funded doctoral research assistant position, which can be renewed up to three years based on satisfactory performance. Interested candidates please contact shichun.huang@unlv.edu for details.
